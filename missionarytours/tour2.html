Route #2 - Buda Tour

A short walk through the major sites of Buda.

Margit-híd -> Margit-sziget -> Árpád-híd -> Óbuda - > Amfiteátrum

  • Duration: 3-4 hours
  • Length: 6 kms walking
  • Starting point: Margit-sziget
  • Ending point: Kolosy tér

 

Starting Point: Margit-sziget

We start on the Margit-híd, where you can take nice photos of the Danube and the two sides.
We will walk through the Margit-sziget from south to north, in a beautiful park, with ancient trees, green grass, and lots of colourful flowers.

 

Waypoint #1: Mini Zoo

There is a mini zoo on the island. In the middle are some storches, you can go an arms length near them.
There are also some horse that can be hand fed. In the other pens you can see animals native to Hungary, from the tamed duck to the white-tailed eagle.

 

Waypoint #2: Szent-Margit Kolostor

Margit-sziget is named after the daughter of King Béla IV., princess Margit of the House of Árpád. Instead of marrying, she reclused into a cloyster on this island.
The cloyster was built at the start of the 13th century. You can walk where the nuns lived and prayed.

 

Waypoint #3: Japanese Garden

Margit-sziget has a thermal bath, and the water used to cool down thermal springs 79 Celsius (175 F) water feeds a small lake.
The lake has water lilies, koi carps and turtles, and the surroundings of the lake is styled like a beautiful japanese garden.

 

Waypoint #4: Óbuda

From the island we cross the Árpád-híd to the Buda side of the river. This part is called Óbuda, for old Buda.
We go through the Szentlélek tér, translated to Holy Spirit square. They preserved the old city center from the 1840s.

 

Waypoint #5: Roman Ruins

They unearthed some roman ruins when they built the bridge. The garrison on the border of the Roman Empire was built in 150 AD.
We can see the ruins of a walled gate, the pool of a roman public bath and some building of the military camp.

 

Ending Point: Amphitheater

We end this tour at the ruins of a roman amphitheater, which was used by the roman legionnaires, to fight with eachother or with animals.
You can climb to the top of the stands and you can go down into the arena.
